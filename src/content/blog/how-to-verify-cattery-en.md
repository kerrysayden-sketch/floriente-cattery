Buying a purebred kitten online is risky. Every year, hundreds of people wire deposits to catteries that don't exist. The money vanishes. The kitten never arrives.

But fake breeders are easy to spot - if you know what to look for. Real catteries leave a paper trail. Here's what to check before you send a single euro.

## The 7-point verification checklist

### 1. Check the cattery registration

Any cattery worth your money is registered with WCF (World Cat Federation), TICA, FIFe, or CFA. Ask for the number. Then go to the organization's website and verify it yourself. Takes two minutes.

### 2. Ask for a video call

A real breeder will show you the kitten on camera - in their home, parents nearby. If they dodge the video call, make excuses, or send "new photos" instead - that's your answer. Move on.

### 3. Verify health testing

Breeders who care test parents for genetic diseases before breeding. For Orientals and Siamese: PRA (progressive retinal atrophy), PK deficiency, blood type. Ask which lab ran the tests. If they can't name one, they probably didn't test.

### 4. Request the contract

No written contract = no deal. A contract should cover health guarantees, spay/neuter terms, return policy, and ownership transfer. If they say "we don't do contracts" - they're not a cattery, they're a seller.

### 5. Check champion titles

Breeders love to mention titles. Champion, Grand Champion, International Champion - each one earned at official shows judged by licensed experts. Ask for proof. Titles are public record.

### 6. Look for real owner reviews

Real names. Real photos of kittens in their new homes. Not stock images, not anonymous quotes. Ask the breeder to connect you with a previous buyer. Anyone who refuses has something to hide.

### 7. Know what's included in the price

Vaccinations, microchip, passport, pedigree, spay/neuter, food starter kit, adaptation guide. A serious breeder lists all of it upfront. If the price is vague or suspiciously low - walk away.

## Red flags: when to walk away

- **100% prepayment required** before you've seen the kitten on video
- **No registration number** or refusal to share it
- **Stock photos** instead of real photos of kittens and parents
- **Price significantly below market** - Oriental/Siamese kittens from registered breeders cost €800-€1,500+ (pet class)
- **No contract offered** or "we don't do contracts"
- **Pressure to decide quickly** - "this kitten won't last" or "deposit today or lose it"
- **No social media presence** - no Instagram, no Facebook, no history of activity

## Before you send money, ask these seven questions

1. What's your cattery registration number? Which organization?
2. Can we do a video call to see the kitten and parents?
3. Which genetic tests have the parents passed? At which lab?
4. Can I see the contract before committing?
5. What titles do the parents have? Where can I verify?
6. Can I talk to a previous buyer?
7. What exactly is included in the price?

A breeder who's done nothing wrong will answer all seven without flinching. Vague answers, stalling, or pushback? Trust your gut. Keep looking.
